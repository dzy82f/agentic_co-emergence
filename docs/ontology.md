\# Agentic Co-Emergence Ontology



This document defines the canonical vocabulary used throughout the Agentic Co-Emergence research programme.



Definitions evolve as the theory evolves.



\---



## Understanding

**Working definition:** Understanding is a structured, evolving, purpose-relative state that organises concepts, relationships, assumptions, evidence, uncertainties, and tensions within a domain, thereby constraining interpretation, inference, and action.

Understanding is not merely the possession of facts or propositions. It is the organised capacity to interpret a domain, relate ideas, recognise uncertainty, revise assumptions, and guide possible action.

In Agentic Co-Emergence, understanding is treated as a first-class computational object. This means it should be capable of being represented, transformed, versioned, inspected, challenged, and empirically evaluated.

Core properties:

* identity
* state
* structure
* domain context
* history
* transformability
* uncertainty
* tension
* perspective
* purpose
